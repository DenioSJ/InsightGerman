Ausbildung is a German term that refers to vocational training or apprenticeship. It is a type of education system in Germany that combines practical training with theoretical instruction and aims to equip individuals with the necessary skills and knowledge to pursue a specific career path.

In the Ausbildung system, young adults typically start training after completing their secondary education and earn a salary during their apprenticeship period. The training period can last from two to three and a half years, depending on the specific occupation.

  • Ausbildung programs are available in various fields, including manufacturing, trade, healthcare, and services.
  • The training is provided by a vocational school, and the practical part is carried out in a company or business.
  • The vocational school provides the theoretical instruction, while the company provides practical training and experience in a real-world work environment.

To work as a nurse in Germany as an international nurse, you need to meet certain requirements and follow specific procedures. Here are the general steps you need to take:

Verify your qualifications: The first step is to check if your nursing degree and qualifications meet the standards and requirements set by the German government. You can do this by contacting the German Federal Nursing Association or the nursing regulatory authority in the state where you wish to work.

  • Learn German: To work as a nurse in Germany, you must be proficient in the German language. You need to pass a language proficiency test, such as the TestDaF or the Deutsche Sprachprüfung für den Hochschulzugang (DSH).
  • Apply for recognition of your nursing qualifications: Once you have verified that your qualifications meet the German standards, you need to apply for recognition of your nursing degree with the competent authority. This process is called the recognition procedure, and it may vary depending on your country of origin.
  • Apply for a visa: After receiving the recognition of your qualifications, you need to apply for a visa that allows you to work in Germany. Depending on your nationality, you may need a visa or a work permit.

Register with the nursing regulatory authority: To work as a nurse in Germany, you need to register with the nursing regulatory authority in the state where you will be working.

A1 Level

The German A1 exam pattern is simple. There are four modules because it is a language: reading, writing, listening, and speaking. It is a 60-point exam, and you must receive 36 points (60%) to pass.

A2 Level

A2 German level refers to above beginner, intermediate level German knowledge. In A2 German, you may simply compose simple sentences and have easy daily discussions.It takes 1 month to complete this section.

B1 Level

B1 When clear standard German is used, German speakers have no trouble comprehending the essential ideas of a conversation, especially when the topic is something familiar, such as school or work.

B2 Level

You'll have roughly 4000 words in your vocabulary. In actuality, the usual criteria at German universities are levels B2 to C1, therefore being able to communicate is sufficient to gain admission to a German-speaking university.

C1 Level

The Goethe-Zertifikat C1 is an adult examination. It attests to candidates' advanced language skills and corresponds to the fifth level (C1) on the six-level competence scale outlined in the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR).
